The Fires of Cali | William Osman

It was a normal day in Ventura County, California. Bright sunny birds, blue sky, happy neighbors. It was early in the morning, and I saw bags packed at the front door, my friend was fearful. Without me needing to ask he pointed to the TV and our house, our street was in the red zone for the fires. I sighed and looked up at the mountain, I saw no fires, but I knew it was coming.

I walked upstairs and put together a bag and grabbed the keys to our RV. I packed very lightly, a few days worth of clothes, drawing supplies, books, my laptop, and a few other bits and bobs. My friend had already grabbed my filled sketchbooks and we went to the garage. Packed the vehicle and ventured out. Parking lot to parking lot until we made it out of the red. However, that was the lesser of the two hells we had to face.
